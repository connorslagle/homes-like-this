GET TO KNOWAurora, CO

What is the best ways to get around in Aurora, CO?

The favoured way to navigate Aurora is very often driving. It is very convenient to park. On the other hand, commuting by public transit is difficult in Aurora. Nonetheless, there is the Peoria Station and 40th Ave & Airport Blvd - Gateway Park Station with access to the A Line. There are around 30 bus lines passing through the city, and bus stops are not especially far-removed from most residences. It is challenging for people walking to navigate Aurora as most daily needs are impractical to meet on foot.

Is Aurora, CO pedestrian friendly?

It is a reasonably short walk to reach primary schools from most homes for sale in Aurora. However, it can be challenging to access high schools as a pedestrian. When it comes to food, some, but not all, property owners in Aurora will live within walking distance of one of over 200 supermarkets. There are also a few choices for those who value close by restaurants and cafes.

What is life in Aurora, CO like?

The character of Aurora is exemplified by its relaxed ambience. There are over 250 green spaces nearby for residents to explore, like Pronghorn Natural Area and Plains Conservation Center, making them easy to reach. This city is quiet overall, as there are low levels of noise from traffic - although there are still several noisier sections, especially near one of the railway lines, Interstate 225, Interstate 70 or one of the 3 airports (Aurora Airpark, Buckley Air Force Base, Front Range Airport).

What are the most popular housing types in Aurora, CO?

The average year of construction for properties in Aurora is 1995. In this city, most dwellings are single detached homes, and the rest are mainly large apartment buildings, small apartment buildings, and townhouses. Roughly 55% of the population of this city own their home and 45% are renters. This city has a good choice of housing size options, ranging from lofts to four or more bedrooms.
